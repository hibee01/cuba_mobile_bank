Project
Background

Cuba is a platform that understands the advantages that digital transformation can bring to the banking and finance industry. It seeks to spearhead the next generation of banking both in Africa and the region, Cuba's goal is to make sure it’s users have access to their money and perform various tasks which includes but not limited to check deposits, bill payments, loan applications, money transfers between accounts from any part of the world etc. The purpose of this project is to build a scalable mobile platform on which to fully digitise all banking services and offerings.

Role in the
project

I was the UI/UX designer for the project, I embarked on the UX research phase, where I conducted ideation workshops, user interviews and ethnographic studies to understand business and user needs and wants. I also created the motion and micro-interaction design for the entire mobile application, ensuring functionality, scalability and consistency across modules to control and elevate the user experience beyond the basics of design and tone of voice.

Research

The goal was to understand the audiences and learn about their backgrounds, demographics, motivations, pain points, emotions and also use a survey to verify how and in what frequency audiences use similar platforms. A competitive analysis paired with previous research gave me more insights to sketch the first prototypes for the consumer app.

Persona

After consolidating and analysing the research data gathered, I came up with 3 personas of fictional users whose goals and characteristics represents the needs of a larger group of users These personas allows me to constantly be reminded of the people that I’m designing the product for, their needs and wants, as well as pain points.

Ideation & Prototype

After generating a broad set of ideas and evaluating them I was able to select the best of them for inclusion into the product. Based on my choice of concept, I created a prototype for user testing. The prototype also allowed me to test navigation and usability internally, as well as quickly coming up with a basic design language to govern how components and layouts should look and flow in different screens.

User Testing

I carried out user testing using the prototype to hear users' feedback on the usability, navigation expected features from a mobile banking application, conduct A, B testing on elements such as icon interpretation, UX writing, and colour palette. Based on the data collected during user testing, I was able to make a couple of iterative improvements in between these main iterations, but they primarily dealt with colour changes, icon style, content display and layout expectations and dashboard personalizations based on individual needs.
